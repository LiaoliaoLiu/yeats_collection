# The Ballad of Moll Magee
- Come round me, little childer;
- There, don’t fling stones at me
- Because I mutter as I go;
- But pity Moll Magee.

<br />

- My man was a poor fisher
- With shore lines in the [say](#5);
- My work was saltin’ herrings
- The whole of the long day.

<br />

- And sometimes from the saltin’ shed
- I scarce could drag my feet,
- Under the blessed moonlight,
- Along the pebbly street.

<br />

- I’d always been but weakly,
- And my baby was just born;
- A neighbour minded her by day,
- I minded her till morn.

<br />

- I lay upon my baby;
- Ye little childer dear,
- I looked on my cold baby
- When the morn grew frosty and clear.

<br />

- A weary woman sleeps so hard!
- My man grew red and pale,
- And gave me money, and bade me go
- To my own place, [Kinsale](#3).

<br />

- He drove me out and shut the door,
- And gave his curse to me;
- I went away in silence,
- No neighbour could I see.

<br />

- The windows and the doors were shut,
- One star shone faint and green,
- The little straws were turnin’ round
- Across the bare [boreen](#4).

<br />

- I went away in silence:
- Beyond old Martin’s byre
- I saw a kindly neighbour
- Blowin’ her mornin’ fire.

<br />

- She drew from me my story—
- My money’s all used up,
- And still, with pityin’, scornin’ eye,
- She gives me bite and sup.

<br />

- She says my man will surely come,
- And fetch me home agin;
- But always, as I’m movin’ round,
- Without doors or within,

<br />

- Pilin’ the wood or pilin’ the turf,
- Or goin’ to the well,
- I’m thinkin’ of my baby
- And [keenin’](../the-ballad-of-father-o%E2%80%99hart/#12) to mysel’.

<br />

- And sometimes I am sure she knows
- When, openin’ wide His door,
- God lights the stars, His candles,
- And looks upon the poor.

<br />

- So now, ye little childer,
- Ye won’t fling stones at me;
- But gather with your shinin’ looks
- And pity Moll Magee.

</PoemSection>

# [茉儿·梅吉谣曲](#1)
- 围到跟前来，小孩子；
- 别因我边走边自语
- 就朝我扔来石头子；
- 要可怜我茉儿·梅吉。

<br />

- 我男人是个穷渔夫，
- 往海里下钓把鱼捉；
- 腌鲱鱼是我的活儿，
- 一天到晚地不停做。

<br />

- 整天都呆在腌鱼棚，
- 我几乎寸步也难挪，
- 有时去有福的月下
- 沿卵石街道踱一踱。

<br />

- 我一向体弱又多病，
- 我的宝宝又刚出生；
- 白天里邻居照看她，
- 夜里我守她到天明。

<br />

- 我压在宝宝的身上；
- 乖乖的孩儿，你们想，
- 到清晨结霜且明亮，
- 看我的宝宝已冰凉。

<br />

- 困乏的女人睡得死！
- 男人脸变红又变白，
- 他给我些钱，教我滚，
- 回我的娘家[金塞尔](#2)。

<br />

- 他撵我出来关上门，
- 背后还送我一顿骂；
- 我一声不响走开去，
- 看不见一个邻人家。

<br />

- 家家户户的门窗闭，
- 只一颗孤星闪绿光，
- 细碎的干草翻滚着
- 掠过那荒凉的小巷。

<br />

- 我一声不响走开去：
- 在老马丁牛栏那边
- 看见个好心的邻居
- 正在吹柴火做早饭。

<br />

- 她问出了我的故事——
- 我的钱已不剩一个，
- 她眼含怜悯和轻贱，
- 还是给了我吃和喝。

<br />

- 她说我男人肯定会
- 来把我重新接回家；
- 可是，我到处忙活着，
- 在人家门里或檐下，

<br />

- 在堆垛木柴或泥炭，
- 或是去井边打水时，
- 总是在想念我宝宝，
- 自个儿伤心得哭泣。

<br />

- 有时我肯定她知道
- 啥时候上帝开天门，
- 点亮那星星，他的灯，
- 照看天下的受苦人。

<br />

- 那么现在呢，小孩子，
- 就不会朝我扔石子；
- 脸蛋儿放光围拢来，
- 而可怜我茉儿·梅吉。

<PoemAppendix source="The Collected Works of W. B. Yeats, Volume I—">
<PoemFootnote>
Yeats claimed that the poem was based on 'a sermon preached in the
chapel at Howth if I remember rightly' (1907; VP p. 843). Howth is a
fishing village on a promontory on the north side of Dublin Bay, several
miles east of the centre of Dublin but inside the city limits; Yeats lived
there from 1881 to 1883.
</PoemFootnote>
<PoemFootnote index={3}>
Kinsale: a seaport in County Cork.
</PoemFootnote>
<PoemFootnote index={4}>
boreen: a boreen (Irish bóithrín, 'little road, lane') is Hiberno-English for a narrow road or lane
</PoemFootnote>
</PoemAppendix>
